Historical SiteMuseum

House of Sampoerna

The House of Sampoerna is a museum dedicated to the heritage of the Sampoerna family and the Indonesian tobacco industry. Located in the old Sampoerna cigarette factory, this Dutch colonial building from 1862 offers visitors a glimpse into Indonesia's tobacco history. The museum features vintage machinery, historical photographs, and guided tours that explain the traditional hand-rolling cigarette process. Visitors can also enjoy the beautiful architecture and learn about the family's contribution to Indonesian culture and economy.

• Opening Hours: Tuesday - Sunday, 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM

• Location: 6 Taman Sampoerna Street, Krembangan Utara

• Admission: Free

Heroes Monument (Tugu Pahlawan)

The Heroes Monument stands as a proud symbol of Surabaya's role in Indonesia's independence struggle. This 41-meter tall concrete monument commemorates the brave heroes who fought in the Battle of Surabaya on November 10, 1945. The monument's design represents a bamboo shoot reaching skyward, symbolizing the people's determination to achieve independence. The underground museum houses historical artifacts, dioramas, and documents from the revolution period, offering visitors an immersive experience of Indonesia's fight for freedom.

• Opening Hours: Tuesday - Sunday, 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM

• Location: Pahlawan Street, Alun-alun Contong

• Admission: IDR 5,000

Surabaya Zoo (KBS)

Established in 1916, Surabaya Zoo is one of Southeast Asia's oldest zoological gardens. Home to over 2,000 animals from 351 species, the zoo features rare Indonesian wildlife including Komodo dragons, Sumatran tigers, orangutans, and various bird species. The 15-hectare park offers educational programs, animal shows, and interactive experiences for families. Recent renovations have improved animal habitats and visitor facilities, making it a perfect destination for wildlife enthusiasts and families looking to learn about biodiversity conservation.

• Opening Hours: Daily, 7:00 AM - 5:00 PM

• Location: 1 Setail Street, Darmo, Wonokromo

• Admission: IDR 15,000 (adults), IDR 10,000 (children)
